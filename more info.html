More on Goan Sausages
Chouricos are spicy pork sausages,
which owe more than a passing debt to Portuguese culinary traditions.
Goan sausages are prepared using well-salted and well-spiced cubes of pork.
Once they have been made,
the strings of sausages dried in the sun and then hung above the fire where they are gradually smoked.
Traditionally they are eaten during the monsoon, when fish is scare.
In preparation, they are soaked in water and then usually fried and served with sauce and rice.
